Carrot Halwa (Gajar Ka Halwa)

Carrot halwa, or gajar ka halwa, is a traditional Indian dessert that's deliciously
rich and creamy. This recipe uses whole milk, not condensed milk. Serve warm
with vanilla ice cream for a real treat!

Prep Time Cook Time Total Time Servings
10 mins 1 hr 1 hr 10 mins 8

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 pounds carrots, grated
  • 1 ½ quarts whole milk
  • 2 cups white sugar
  • ½ cup raisins or sultanas
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cardamom
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 8 tablespoons chopped pistachio nuts

Directions

  1. First Step

    Heat ghee in a large saucepan over medium heat and add carrots. Cook
    and stir briefly, about 3 minutes. Pour in milk and increase heat; bring
    to a boil and cook, stirring constantly, for 5 minutes. Reduce heat to low
    and simmer gently, uncovered, and stirring occasionally until moisture
    evaporates, about 1 hour.

  2. Next Step

    Bring carrot mixture back to a boil; add sugar, raisins, cardamom, and
    saffron. Cook and stir until mixture has thickened, about 10 minutes.

  3. Final Step

    Ladle carrot halwa into 8 individual serving dishes. Top each serving
    with 1 tablespoon pistachios. Serve warm or chill before serving.

Pro Tip

Use a hand grater to prepare the carrots. Using a food processor makes them
too watery.
